Capital Structure

Hodl Holdings adopts a layered capital architecture anchored in Bitcoin as the base asset. This structure is designed to ensure patrimonial stability, legal predictability, and long-term funding flexibility.

Our corporate capital stack is composed of:

  • Base: Bitcoin in segregated custody, serving as the reserve backing of the operation.
  • Level 1: Common shares with voting rights and strategic control of the company.
  • Level 2: Preferred shares with dividend priority.
  • Top layer: Structured debt instruments, such as:
    • Simple or convertible debentures
    • Commercial notes
    • Structured financing backed by Bitcoin-linked guarantees

This capital pyramid allows risk modulation and incentive alignment between founders, investors, and creditors, respecting liquidation and return seniority across scenarios.

Governance

Hodl Holdings adopts a corporate governance model focused on multigenerational continuity, strategic control, and transparency among stakeholders.

Key decisions are made by common shareholders, recorded in formal meeting minutes, with veto rights outlined in the shareholder agreement. The issuance of debt instruments or disposal of bitcoins requires collective approval.

The structure includes succession planning mechanisms, protection of the company's original mission, and clear separation between controlling and investing capital.
